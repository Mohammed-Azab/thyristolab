\subsection{Full-Wave Bridge Rectifier Results}

The bridge configuration uses four thyristors and provides performance similar to the center-tapped design but with a standard (non-center-tapped) transformer.

\subsubsection{Charging Time Analysis}

Figure \ref{fig:bridge_time} illustrates how charging time varies with firing angle for the bridge rectifier. The relationship is similar to other configurations, with charging time increasing exponentially as the firing angle approaches $90^\circ$ due to reduced average output voltage and current.

Figure \ref{fig:bridge_losses} shows power losses as a function of firing angle. \subsection{Comparative Analysis}

\subsubsection{Performance Comparison}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Half-Wave}: Simplest (one thyristor), slowest charging, highest ripple
    \item \textbf{Full-Wave CT}: Two thyristors + center-tapped transformer, 50\% faster charging, reduced ripple
    \item \textbf{Full-Wave Bridge}: Four thyristors + standard transformer, similar performance to CT, no center-tap required
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Key Observations}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Firing angle provides flexible control of charging current
    \item Low firing angles ($\alpha < 60^\circ$) enable fast charging but higher losses
    \item Full-wave configurations significantly superior for practical applications
    \item Thyristor forward drop has measurable impact on performance
    \item SoC tracking enables intelligent charging strategies
\end{itemize}
